TECHNICAL MEMORANDUM                                         TBX-26-009

SUBJECT:      Ack silence means the link is dead - failing over in two
              seconds instead of freezing through the zombie window
DATE:         2026-07-16
STATUS:       FINAL - diagnosed from three identical journal episodes;
              fix deployed, proves itself on every box-side link drop


SUMMARY. Days after the ack-paced frame pushes shipped (TBX-26-008),
the operator reported the visualizer occasionally freezing while "a
short other display" appeared - suspected to be a notification. It was
neither a notification nor a regression in the pacing itself: the box
drops its BLE link on its own roughly every 39 minutes, and for about
30 seconds afterwards BlueZ keeps accepting writes into the dead
connection before it notices. The ack pacing turned that window into
fourteen consecutive 2-second timeouts, each patiently logged, while
the panel sat frozen and the box fell back to its own clock pages.
The journal shows ack silence has exactly one cause here, so the fix
is one line of policy: the first missed ack now raises, handing the
stream to the existing reconnect path within ~2 seconds.

1. DECODING THE SYMPTOM. Three episodes since the TBX-26-008 deploy,
byte-for-byte alike in the journal: about fourteen "no frame ack in
2s" lines two seconds apart, then the frame write itself failing with
"Service Discovery has not been performed yet" (bleak's way of saying
the disconnect event finally landed), then a clean redial some five
seconds later. Total outage ~35 seconds. The "short other display" is
the box itself: once its link dies it falls back to its current
channel - the clock, whose page cycle includes the animated weather
scene - and the ~3-second init handshake on reconnect keeps it there
a moment longer before the next frame repaints the tunnel.

2. THE 39-MINUTE CADENCE. The idle log settles the cause: the box has
been dropping the LE link on a ~39-minute rhythm for days, music or
no music - each weather-loop pass finds the link gone and redials.
This is firmware behavior, not load; nothing the daemon sends provokes
it and nothing it sends can prevent it. The only lever is how fast the
daemon notices.

3. THE ZOMBIE WINDOW. The box kills its side without anything reaching
BlueZ, and until the kernel declares the connection dead (~30 s),
write-without-response keeps "succeeding" into the void. TBX-26-008
established that acks normally return within ~150 ms even with the
link saturated. The old fire-and-forget code streamed invisibly into
this window (panel frozen either way); the ack-paced code made the
daemon WAIT through it, two seconds per frame, holding the panel lock
- politely logging a warning per frame and carrying on. Thirty-plus
hours of journal contain 42 such warnings, every single one part of a
death storm, zero singletons: a lost-but-harmless ack has never been
observed in paced operation.

4. THE FIX. _push_frame() now raises BleakError("no frame ack in 2s")
where it previously logged and returned. The endless visualizer's
except-BleakError path - already proven by every prior link loss -
drops the client and redials, and connect_le's kick actively
terminates the box's zombie side rather than waiting out the kernel.
Outage per drop shrinks from ~35 s to ~5-9 s. Timed runs die on the
same raise, consistent with their existing policy of not overrunning
the window. Journal signature of a handled drop: "panel write failed
(no frame ack in 2s) - reconnecting", then "LE reconnected".

5. DISPOSITION. Fixed in timebox_daemon.py (one raise in _push_frame);
verified streaming clean after deploy under worst-case broadband
noise; the failover re-verifies itself on the box's next 39-minute
drop. The lesson refines TBX-26-008: the ack is the link's heartbeat,
and its absence is as informative as its arrival. A timeout that is
logged and walked past is a decision to stay wedged - when the data
says the silence has exactly one cause, act on the first one.
