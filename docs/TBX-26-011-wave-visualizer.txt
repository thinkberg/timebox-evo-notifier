TECHNICAL MEMORANDUM                                         TBX-26-011

SUBJECT:      Wave visualizer - scrolling 16-band spectrogram with
              selectable axis, palette, and stereo center-out flow
DATE:         2026-07-16
STATUS:       FINAL - feature operational, validated on hardware


SUMMARY. The visualizer gained a third mode: a wave. Each frame's 16
band heights become one line of pixels; the history scrolls across
the panel, dimming with age. The scroll axis is a knob: "h" enters at
the right edge and drifts left (low bands at the bottom, like the
bars), "v" enters at the top row and falls (low bands at the left).
In stereo the two channels enter at the middle and age outward -
left toward the left/bottom edge, right toward the right/top, the
same L/R split as the stereo bars - halving the visible history to 8
frames per side. Two palettes: "rainbow" (hue = band, brightness =
level, the tunnel's color function) and "heat" (green quiet, yellow
mid, red loud, the bars' palette read as intensity). Mode, dir, and
palette are FIFO parameters, switchable while running. The mode is a
structural clone of the tunnel (TBX-26-005): a history deque, pure
frame functions, fade^age through the same 32-step quantizer, and
the history cleared at every capture-gap site.

1. DESIGN.

   1.1 Geometry. A deque(maxlen=16) holds per-frame heights, newest
   first. Mono paints entry `age` at column 15-age (dir h) or row
   `age` (dir v). Stereo stores (L, R) tuples and paints L at column
   7-age / row 8+age, R at column 8+age / row 7-age; entries past
   age 7 fall off the panel. Analysis is unchanged bars machinery:
   16 log-spaced Goertzel bands, rows=16 even in stereo (the value
   is intensity, not bar height), shared auto-gain peak across
   channels. The tunnel-only `bands` knob does not apply.

   1.2 Color and fade. rainbow reuses _tunnel_color (hue = band,
   brightness = sqrt(height/16), silence black); heat reuses
   _bar_color on the height (black at 0). Position already names the
   band, so heat trades the redundant hue for a loudness read. Aging
   lines dim by fade^age (the existing knob, default 0.92; at age 15
   that is ~0.29, still visible) through _q32, keeping a worst-case
   dense frame within the 6-chunk BLE budget of TBX-26-008 - pinned
   by the same runnable check as the tunnel.

   1.3 Runtime knobs. mode gained "wave"; dir ("h"/"v") and palette
   ("rainbow"/"heat") are new FIFO keys, whitelisted like mode,
   seeded at start, and applied live to a running visualizer.

2. TRAPS.

   2.1 Live stereo toggle vs. history shape. The one deque holds
   lists (mono) or (L, R) tuples (stereo); flipping stereo while
   running leaves the other shape in the history, and painting a
   tuple as heights would die on comparison with int. Each renderer
   stops at the first mismatched entry - the history visibly
   truncates for up to 16 frames after a toggle, then refills. The
   alternative (clearing the deque from the request handler) spreads
   render state into the FIFO path for no visible gain.

   2.2 Shared gap-clearing. The tunnel cleared its history at four
   capture-gap sites; a second mode doubles that to eight scattered
   clears, and a missed one means stale spectra replay on resume.
   One helper (_clear_vis_history) now owns all visualizer history;
   the four sites call it.

3. DISPOSITION. Validated per TBX-26-004 practice: geometry,
palette, stereo symmetry, silence, shape-mismatch, and encode-size
invariants as runnable checks (test_render.py); mode (bars/wave/
tunnel, both directions), dir, palette, and stereo switched live
over the FIFO against a running AirPlay stream on hardware, journal
clean throughout. The capture pause/resume path is unchanged code;
its wave history-clear rides the shared helper and is pinned by the
runnable checks rather than re-driven live (real audio was playing).
