TECHNICAL MEMORANDUM                                         TBX-26-007

SUBJECT:      The clock channel decoded - page cycling, command layout,
              and reading the box's mind with 0x46 state dumps
DATE:         2026-07-14
STATUS:       FINAL - every claim below verified against the panel by
              state-dump readback


SUMMARY. What looked like a stuck "demo mode" - the idle panel jumping
through animated pages - was the clock channel doing exactly what it
was told. Each enabled sub-view (time, weather, temperature, date) is
a separate FULL-SCREEN page the box rotates every ~15 seconds, and the
weather page is an animated scene; enabling "clock and weather" buys
the carousel, the firmware cannot merge pages. Chasing this produced a
partial decode of the 0x46 state dump, settled which of two
conflicting community layouts of the clock command this firmware
speaks, and caught two traps: the bare channel switch RESETS the
stored sub-views, and a silently-swallowed exception in our own
restore path sent the investigation down two wrong theories before the
journal revealed the operator had simply changed the setting.

1. THE 0x46 STATE DUMP. Sending opcode 0x46 returns a state frame the
library leaves undecoded. Diffing dumps across known writes mapped
enough of it to be useful (16x16 Evo, firmware as of 2026-07):

    01 1900 04 46 55 CH .. .. .. .. BR .. .. .. .. RR GG BB
                                          ?? T W P D SUM .. 02

    CH   channel (00 clock, 02 cloud - matches the 45 arguments)
    BR   brightness (0x50 = 80)
    RGB  clock color
    T W P D   sub-view flags: time, weather, temp, date
    ??/..     unmapped; SUM moves with content (checksum)

The dump answers "what does the box THINK it is showing" - ground
truth no amount of staring at the panel provides. Acks observed: a 45
write returns `45 55 00` plus an unsolicited `31 55 ...` notification.

2. CLOCK COMMAND LAYOUT. Two community sources disagree on the clock
channel switch. State readback settles it for this firmware:

    45 00 01 FACE T W P D RR GG BB      <- correct (node-divoom layout)
    45 00    FACE T W P D RR GG BB      <- MISPARSES here

Writing time+weather/white via the first form lands exactly [T=1 W=1]
with a clean white color. The second form shifts everything by one:
color read back corrupted (ffff7f), flags scrambled. And the bare
2-byte `45 00` is NOT "switch and keep settings": it RESETS the stored
sub-view flags (observed landing on time-only once and all-zero
another time). The daemon therefore always sends the full 11-byte form
with explicit flags, never the bare switch.

3. WHAT PERSISTS. The sub-view flags survive BLE disconnect, reconnect
and box power (NV-stored); a 5F weather-data push does NOT flip the
weather flag on. The boot channel is set by `8A 01 <channel>` (also
NV) - pinning it to 00 stops the out-of-box demo carousel after a
power cycle. Weather data itself is lost on power-off, which is why
the daemon re-pushes 5F every cycle rather than only on fetch.

4. THE INVESTIGATION, AND WHERE IT WENT WRONG. Three theories fell in
sequence: (a) "the weather sub-view is the demo" - refuted when
time-only was set and the operator still saw cycling; (b) "the box
reverts flags on disconnect" - refuted by a write/disconnect/reconnect
probe; (c) the truth, found in the journal: a FIFO request had
re-enabled time+weather between our write and the observation, and the
pages were cycling as configured. Two of our own defects stretched
this hunt: the restore helper swallowed every exception silently, so
"the daemon set time-only at startup" was an assumption, not a fact
(TBX-26-004 again - a silent failure path is an unverified outcome);
and the in-memory clock choice was reset by every daemon restart,
which the operator correctly reported as "does not stick" (now a
TIMEBOX_CLOCK env default).

5. DISPOSITION. The daemon speaks the verified 11-byte clock command
exclusively, defaults its pages from TIMEBOX_CLOCK (falling back to
time,weather), and re-asserts them on every restore. The probe
scripts' method - write, read the state dump back, diff - is the
standard for any future opcode work: the panel shows you a picture,
but 0x46 tells you the state.
