TECHNICAL MEMORANDUM                                         TBX-26-010

SUBJECT:      Silence with a running sink - the black-panel hours, and
              why the clock now takes the panel back on quiet audio
DATE:         2026-07-16
STATUS:       FINAL - reproduced live during diagnosis; both
              transitions verified against the panel with timestamps


SUMMARY. The operator reported the box "just stopped" - and unlike the
morning's episodes (TBX-26-009), the journal for the window was
completely empty: no ack timeouts, no link losses, radio links healthy
throughout. The box had not stopped; it was diligently displaying an
endless stream of black frames. The visualizer's idle detection rested
on the sink's RUNNING state, and this system always has a silent but
uncorked stream on the default sink (the combine-sink slave; an
AirPlay sender left on pause). Silence therefore never looked idle:
the silence counter just respawned the capture, and the frames it kept
rendering were black. A pixel panel showing black is indistinguishable
from a dead one. The silence trip now behaves like the idle
transition: clock restored, frames withheld until real samples arrive.

1. A FAILURE WITH NO LOG LINES. Between the last "audio capture
resumed" and the operator giving up and stopping the service, the
journal shows only routine clock flashes and weather pushes. Every
radio layer was ruled out: wireplumber saw the A2DP transport running
uninterrupted, and with the TBX-26-009 code deployed a dead LE link
cannot pass silently any more. What remained was a daemon doing
exactly what it was told: capture zeros, render zeros, push zeros -
ten black frames a second, acked by the box, forever. The one comment
in the code that knew ("panel is black either way") had classified
this as a harmless ceiling. It was user-visible the whole time.

2. WHY SILENCE NEVER LOOKED IDLE. The endless visualizer pauses its
capture when the default sink stops RUNNING - correct for players
that cork their streams when stopped, which is how it always worked in
testing. But this system's default sink permanently hosts an uncorked
slave stream from a combine sink, and Shairport Sync keeps its stream
open while the AirPlay sender is merely paused. Either one holds the
sink RUNNING while feeding exact digital zeros. The silence counter
(10 s of all-zero frames) existed for precisely this case - but its
only action was to respawn the capture so the sink-state check could
run again, and the check kept answering RUNNING. The loop rendered on.

3. THE FIX. The silence trip is now a first-class idle transition:
restore the clock, log "audio silent - clock restored", clear the
tunnel history, and stop pushing frames until a nonzero sample
arrives ("audio capture resumed"). A notification overlay still
paints - on a black background - while silent, matching the paused
path. The capture keeps cycling every 10 s underneath, so the
mic-in-use indicator stays on while a silent stream holds the sink;
that remains the (now genuinely cosmetic) ceiling.

4. VERIFICATION, WITH A DETOUR. The condition reproduced itself during
diagnosis: the operator's audio went quiet mid-investigation and the
freshly deployed trip fired on schedule - the panel went to the clock,
not to black. The detour is instructive: with the visualizer silently
waiting, the daemon's CPU use dropped so low that it read as a wedged
process, and two capture respawns overlapping made it look like a
process leak. Both were the waiting loop working as designed. A
20-second noise burst then pinned both directions: "audio capture
resumed" one second after sound started, "audio silent - clock
restored" 18 s after it ended (the counter counts render iterations,
not wall seconds, so ack pacing stretches the nominal 10 s somewhat).

5. DISPOSITION. Fixed in timebox_daemon.py (the silence trip in
visualize()); README updated where it described the old pause
behavior. Two lessons. First, an idle detector needs to believe the
data, not just the plumbing: sink state says someone MAY be playing,
only the samples say whether they ARE. Second, a known limitation
filed as harmless deserves one test against reality - "panel is black
either way" was true, and the panel being black was exactly the bug.
